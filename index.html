Local Protest Guide: Alma & St. Louis Region

Your 90-minute safety & support playbook

Protest Guide

Preparation Essentials

  • Hydration & Snacks – pack water and easy-to-eat fuel.
  • Protest Flyers – hand out “Protest Flyers” with a link to this guide. Extras from the site or DNC office.
  • Livestream & Recording – use your phone’s audio recorder if you don’t have a body camera.
  • Weather & Dress – check the forecast; wear layers, rain gear, sunscreen as needed.

Safety & Grouping

  • Small teams (2–3) for backup and mutual watch.
  • Larger groups split into mini-teams at different corners.

Sign Design & Messaging

  • Invite support with icons (horn, raised fist, soundwaves).
  • Match every honk or cheer: thumbs-up, fist pump, or shout “Thank you!”.

Quick Sign-Making Guide

  1. Materials: 18×24″ cardboard, construction paper, strong tape, wooden dowel.
  2. Layer contrasting paper, tape all edges, attach dowel securely, sleeve in plastic if you can.

Order waterproof signs online

Engagement & Energy

Stay positive—respond to every supportive gesture to keep momentum high.

Timing & Duration

Weekdays 4 PM–sunset (peak 4–6 PM). Limit your shift to about 90 minutes so energy stays strong.

Confrontations & Hecklers

  • If someone approaches aggressively, hold your sign between you and them and stay silent.
  • Ignore hecklers. Your calm focus on supporters is the strongest statement.
