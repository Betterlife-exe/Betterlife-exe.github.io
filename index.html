Alma & St. Louis Local Protest Guide

Stand firm, stay safe, and connect with protest partners.

Preparation Essentials

1. Hydration & Snacks: Bring water and easy snacks to stay energized.
2. Protest Flyers: Distribute "Protest Flyers" with a link to this guide; extras printable from site or DNC office.
3. Livestream & Recording: Livestream on social media. If no body cam, use your phone’s audio recorder or small recorder.
4. Weather & Dress: Check forecast. Dress in layers; wear rain gear, hat, sunscreen as needed.

Safety & Grouping

• Small teams (2–3) provide backup and support.
• Larger groups split into mini-teams across corners.

Sign Design & Messaging

• Invite support with icons (horn, fist, soundwaves) + clear text asking for honks or raised fists.
• Match energy: acknowledge cheers with a thumbs-up, fist pump, or "thank you!".

Quick Sign-Making Guide

  1. Materials: 18"×24" cardboard, construction paper, tape/glue, wooden dowel or PVC pole.
  2. Build:
    • Cut cardboard, layer on construction paper.
    • Reinforce edges with tape.
    • Attach handle; wrap tape around joint.
    • Optional: cover with plastic or clear tape for weatherproofing.

Engagement & Energy

Match honks/cheers with raised signs, calls of "thank you", or noise.

Timing & Duration

Weekdays, 4:00pm–sunset. Peak: 4:00–6:00pm. Limit to 90 minutes.

Confrontations & Hecklers

• For aggressive pedestrians: hold sign between you and them, stay silent.
• Ignore hecklers aiming for reaction.

Recommended Streets

  • Main Ave
  • Commercial Rd
  • Oak Street
  • Pine Boulevard
