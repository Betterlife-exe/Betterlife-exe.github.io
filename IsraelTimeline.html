Historical Timeline of the Israel Region (4000 BC – 2000 AD)

4000–3000 BC – Early Settlement

Neolithic farmers and Chalcolithic Ghassulian culture appear with early copper tools and farming.

3000–2000 BC – Proto-Canaanite Culture

Urban city-states emerge. Eastern migrants blend with locals forming early Canaanite identity.

2000–1200 BC – Middle & Late Bronze Age

Canaanite states flourish under Egyptian oversight; rise of Philistines and early Israelite identity begins.

1200–586 BC – Iron Age Kingdoms

United Monarchy of Israel; divided into Israel and Judah; Assyrian and Babylonian conquest follows.

586–332 BC – Exile & Return

Babylonian exile; return under Persia; Second Temple built in Jerusalem.

332–63 BC – Hellenistic Rule

Alexander’s conquest spreads Greek culture; Maccabean revolt leads to Hasmonean Jewish autonomy.

63 BC–70 AD – Roman Rule

Rome conquers Judea; Jewish revolts; Second Temple destroyed in 70 AD.

70–638 AD – Byzantine & Early Rabbinic Period

Christianization under Byzantines; Rabbinic Judaism and Talmud take form in Galilee.

638–1099 AD – Islamic Caliphates

Arab Muslim rule; construction of Dome of the Rock and Al-Aqsa; Jewish communities return.

1099–1517 AD – Crusaders to Mamluks

Crusader Kingdom; Muslim reconquest under Saladin; Mamluk control in later period.

1517–1917 AD – Ottoman Empire

Relative stability; rise of Safed as center of Kabbalah; 19th-century Jewish immigration increases.

1917–1948 AD – British Mandate

Balfour Declaration supports Jewish homeland; waves of immigration; Arab-Jewish conflict intensifies.

1948–2000 AD – State of Israel

Establishment of Israel; wars with neighbors; Palestinian displacement; peace treaties and modern tensions.
